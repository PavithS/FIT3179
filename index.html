Global Immigration (1990-2019)

A visualisation exploring global immigration by country, continent, development and gender.

Where are immigrants travelling?

Asia and Europe consistently intake the highest number of immigrants, which is possibly influenced by high migration within the continents due to Asia containing 59.54% of the global population[1] and the freedom of movement in the EU[2].

Due to the large population in Asia, immigration per capita in the region remains lower than other regions; China is the lowest globally with 0.7 per capita in 2019. North America also receives a large portion of global immigration largely due to the US being one of the most popular tourist destinations[3].

Countries in the Middle-East lead immigration per capita by a sizeable margin, which is sustained by their large and growing migrant worker population[4].
